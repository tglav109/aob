It Can Be That Simple

One breath changed your state just now.
Imagine what a week could do.

You felt it with the circle above. A few breaths in, something shifted. That's not a trick — it's physiology. And most people who want to help others with breath already have the heart for it. What's often missing is the understanding of why it works, and the confidence to use it when it matters.

The Calm Code teaches that. How the body actually breathes, why patterns break down, and what you can do about it — simply and safely. Not cathartic breathwork. Not altered states. The everyday science of breathing and regulation.

The training starts with your own nervous system — that's the foundation. Everything else builds from there. By the end of the week, you leave with tools you trust and the felt confidence to use them.

The Curriculum

50 hours. Four pillars.
One complete foundation.

40 residential hours at ASHA Retreat Centre in Tuscany, plus a 10-hour online component you complete before arrival.

01

Pillar One

Mechanics

How the body is designed to breathe, and what gets in the way. You explore the structural foundations through hands-on practice with fellow participants.

  • Nasal breathing function and restoration
  • Diaphragm assessment and activation
  • Ribcage mobility and accessory breathing
  • Postural integration across positions
02

Pillar Two

Chemistry

Why over-breathing creates dysregulation, and why CO2 tolerance matters more than most people realize. Taught simply, with safety first — building understanding, not overwhelm.

  • CO2 tolerance and why it matters
  • Over-breathing pattern recognition
  • Anxiety physiology: the breath connection
  • Gas exchange and oxygen delivery basics
03

Pillar Three

Cadence

The rhythm and pacing of breath that shifts how the nervous system responds. You experience each practice in your own body first, then with a partner.

  • Coherence breathing and resonance
  • Core techniques: box breath, 4-7-8, transformer breath
  • How breath pacing affects the autonomic nervous system
  • Vagal tone and heart-rate variability
04

Pillar Four

Application

Where understanding becomes confidence. Through partner practice, role-play, and feedback cycles, you learn what it feels like to guide someone toward calmer breathing.

  • The 90-second rapid reset protocol
  • The 12-minute daily regulation practice
  • Breath assessment and partner practice
  • Holding space and the basics of coaching with breath

Before You Arrive

The 10-hour online pre-study covers foundational respiratory anatomy and breathing science. Completing it before the residential week means your time at ASHA is spent on practice and applied sessions, not theory alone.

Who This Is For

who work with people.

The Calm Code is designed for people who already work with clients and want a science-informed breath method to add to their practice. No prior breathwork training is required. And because the training starts with your own regulation, many participants also experience it as a deep personal reset.

This training does not cover

Conscious connected breathwork delivery, trauma catharsis, somatic release, or altered-state practices. You'll experience a conscious connected breathwork session during the week, but teaching it is covered in Weeks 2 and 3 of the facilitator training, which Calm Code graduates can enter directly.

Why This Exists

A different kind of breathwork training.

Most breathwork trainings focus on cathartic or expanded-state experiences. That is valuable and beautiful work. The Calm Code focuses on something complementary: the everyday physiology of breathing, and how understanding it can help you support people in a simple, grounded way.

You cannot guide someone else to calm if you haven't found it in your own body first. That's why the week starts there.
